Q1:
Which of the following are amongst the vaccine-preventable diseases endemic in Indonesia?
{{question1.correctlyAnswered?"That's correct.":"That's incorrect."}}
The correct answer is:
Rabies, hepatitis B and hepatitis A.
1,2

Both hepatitis B and A are endemic in Indonesia and both are vaccine-preventable diseases. Authorities (AIH, CDC and WHO) recommend that non-immune travellers should be vaccinated against both diseases when travelling to Indonesia.1-4

Rabies is another disease that is endemic in Indonesia, with authorities (AIH, CDC and WHO) recommending pre-exposure (PreP) rabies vaccination for travellers with an increased risk of exposure to animals in countries like Indonesia. Children are especially vulnerable, because they tend to play with animals, might not report bites, and are more likely to have animal bites around the head and neck.2-4 When the clinical symptoms of rabies appear, the disease is fatal with very few exceptions.2,3

So, the Robertson family should also be vaccinated against rabies before travelling.1-4

Q3:
Which of the following statements is false?
{{question3.correctlyAnswered?"That's correct.":"That's incorrect."}}
The correct answer is:
The false statement is "Rabies immunoglobulin is readily accessible in developing countries."

Rabies vaccine and rabies immunoglobulin (RIG) is often difficult to access in developing countries. All travellers should be informed that a worldwide shortage of RIG means that it may not be readily available at their destination and that safety may not be guaranteed.1-3 Travellers to rabies at-risk regions may be required to travel to an alternative destination in order to access appropriate post-exposure treatment.2

Travellers should also be educated to avoid animal exposure. Following potential exposure to rabies, travellers should immediately wash all animal bites, licks, and scratches with soap and water and seek medical care as soon as possible.1-3
